# MSP product page with premium calculator

MSP (Manulife Saving Protector) gets a full product page following the established pattern: hero, advantages grid, benefits cards, premium tables, a benefit calculator with year-by-year breakdown, FAQ accordion, and CTA. The calculator uses a lookup-table design (`MSP_DATA` object) that encodes minimum premiums, modal factors, and per-term benefit percentages for both IDR and USD. The death benefit model introduces separate percentage rates for natural and accidental death in the post-premium period, with ADB cap enforcement.

Watch for: Death benefit formula in the calculator diverges from the Sample reference's illustration examples (confirmed). Year-by-year table shows death benefit = `totalManfaatHidup` for years 1-5 proportional, but the Sample describes it as proportional to "sisa tunai + manfaat akhir" which produces different numbers (confirmed). `innerHTML` used for calculator output without an explicit safety comment documenting the sanitization invariant (confirmed, low-risk).

## High-level view

The death benefit calculation for years 1-5 uses `proporsional * totalManfaatHidup` as the basis, while the Sample reference material uses a proportional factor applied to "sisa pembayaran tunai yang belum diterima + manfaat akhir" (remaining unpaid annual cash + maturity benefit). These produce different numbers: Rp 354M vs Rp 336M for the same year-3 death scenario. The new calculator's formula for post-premium years (Year 6+) introduces `persen_meninggal_alami_pasca_premi` and `persen_meninggal_kecelakaan_pasca_premi` rates (0.25/0.35 for IDR 10-year) that are not present in the Sample, suggesting either source material the Sample didn't have, or a misinterpretation.

Year 5 in the year-by-year table simultaneously credits a tunai payment AND a 100% death benefit. Since the FAQ states tunai is paid "di akhir tahun polis ke-5," death during year 5 may preclude that payment, making the table potentially misleading.

## Death benefit formula divergence from reference material

The calculator's death benefit computation for years 1-5 differs from the Sample's illustrated examples. The Sample (`Sample/Msp.html`) provides a concrete illustration for IDR, 10-year, Rp 100M annual premium:

```
Meninggal Tahun ke-3 (sakit):
  60% x (sisa 4 pembayaran tunai Rp15jt + Rp500jt akhir)
  = 60% x Rp 560.000.000
  = Rp 336.000.000
```

The new calculator computes year 3 as:
```
proporsional = 3 * 0.20 = 0.60
meninggalAlamiYr = 0.60 * totalManfaatHidup
totalManfaatHidup = (0.15 * 100M * 6) + (5 * 100M) = 90M + 500M = 590M
meninggalAlamiYr = 0.60 * 590M = 354.000.000
```

The two implementations disagree by Rp 18M for year-3 death. The discrepancy stems from whether the proportional factor is applied to `totalManfaatHidup` (all 6 tunai + maturity = 590M) or to a subset ("sisa 4 pembayaran tunai" + maturity = 560M). The Sample says "sisa 4" for a year-3 death in a 10-year term, which is itself puzzling since no tunai has been paid yet at year 3 (first payment is end of year 5). This may reflect a contractual interpretation where death during the premium period cancels 2 future payment years. The source PDF should be the arbiter.

For the post-premium period (Year 6+), the divergence is larger:

```
New calculator at Year 6 (IDR/10yr):
  meninggalAlamiYr = (0.25 * 100M * 5) + 500M = 625M

Sample at Year 6 (IDR/10yr):
  100% x (sisa 4 tunai Rp15jt + Rp500jt) = 560M
```

The new calculator uses `persenMeninggalAlami` (0.25) where the Sample uses `persenTunai` (0.15) for the remaining-payment component. The `persen_meninggal_alami_pasca_premi` rates suggest a different source (possibly the training PDF's death benefit table which may show higher per-year values that bake in a separate benefit structure). This needs verification because the formula structures are fundamentally different, not just the rates.

## Year 5 tunai and death benefit overlap

Year 5 in the year-by-year table shows `statusYr = 'Masa Premi'`, credits `tunaiYr = manfaatTunaiPerTahun`, and computes death benefit as `1.0 * totalManfaatHidup`. The table presents these side-by-side, implying that a policyholder who dies during year 5 both receives the tunai for that year and the full death benefit. Since the product FAQ states tunai is paid "di akhir tahun polis ke-5," a death mid-year-5 should not trigger the tunai payment. The death benefit column at year 5 should probably reflect that the year-5 tunai is still part of the "remaining unpaid" amount rather than being separately credited.

## Mode-change minimum premium edge case

When a user enters a value, changes the payment mode to one with a higher minimum, and doesn't interact with the input field again, `calculateMSP()` fires and shows an error message but does not auto-correct the input value. Auto-correction only happens via `handleMspBlur`. This means the user sees "below minimum" but the stale value persists in the input field. Minor UX friction, not a correctness issue.
