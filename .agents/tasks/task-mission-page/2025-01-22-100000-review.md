# MiSSION Syariah product page with contribution calculator

A new product page for MiSmart Insurance Solution Syariah (MiSSION Syariah) following the established mifip.html pattern: hero with stat cards, product overview grid, benefit cards, contribution tables, a premium calculator with lookup-based validation, 10-item FAQ accordion, CTA section, and floating WhatsApp button. The calculator is deliberately simplified compared to the Sample/Mission.html reference (which includes age-based rate interpolation, rider selection, and multi-currency support); this version validates contribution minimums and UP bounds, then shows allocation breakdowns and loyalty bonus projections. Content uses bahasa pertanyaan (question-based language) throughout.

Watch for: The calculator computes loyalty bonuses using the annual equivalent of per-period contributions (kontribusi × multiplier), but the product terms specify loyalty is based on "kontribusi dasar tahun pertama" — if a user increases their contribution after year 1, the calculator would overstate the bonus (likely). The COI reference card shows hardcoded values for age 30 but presents them alongside dynamic calculator results, which could mislead users into thinking COI scales with their inputs (confirmed).

## High-level view

The page is a mechanical port of the mifip.html structure with mission-specific content and styling variables. CSS custom properties (`--mission-green`, `--mission-dark`, `--mission-light`) use the same jade green palette as mifip. All component class names are properly namespaced with `mission-` prefix to avoid collisions.

The calculator validates three constraints: minimum contribution per payment mode (lookup table), minimum UP as `max(100M, 5× annual equivalent)`, and maximum UP of 10B. The design intentionally avoids the complex age/gender-based rate interpolation of the Sample reference, treating it as a projection tool rather than a quoting engine. The static COI card placed inside the dynamic results wrapper creates a false visual association between hardcoded age-30 values and the user's actual inputs.

The monthly minimum (Rp 340,000) × 12 = Rp 4,080,000, which the calculator displays as "Kontribusi Tahunan (Ekuivalen)" — contradicting the FAQ and hero stat that both describe the product as "Rp 4 juta/tahun." This is correct modal-factor pricing, but the marketing copy doesn't acknowledge the 2% difference.

<summary>Issues (3)</summary>

1. **Loyalty bonus basis ambiguity** — The calculator applies the 50%/700% loyalty percentages to `annualKontribusi` (current contribution × frequency multiplier). Product terms define it as "kontribusi dasar tahun pertama" which is a fixed historical value. If the user is modeling a contribution higher than what they'd actually commit to in year 1, or if they increase contributions later, the projection is overstated. Add a note in the results or use explicit labeling like "berdasarkan kontribusi tahun pertama sebesar X" to set expectations.
2. **Static COI card mixed with dynamic results** — The "Referensi COI (Usia 30, per 1M UP)" card renders inside `missionResultsWrap` alongside computed results. Users may not notice the "Usia 30" qualifier and assume the COI figures relate to their specific profile. Move the COI card outside the dynamic results container or add stronger visual separation (e.g., a disclaimer banner above it).
3. **Monthly minimum × 12 exceeds annual minimum** — Rp 340,000 × 12 = Rp 4,080,000, which is Rp 80,000 more than the annual minimum of Rp 4,000,000. The FAQ states "sekitar Rp 340.000 per bulan" implying equivalence, and the hero shows "Rp 4jt/thn." The calculator contradicts both by showing "Ekuivalen: Rp 4.080.000" for monthly users. Clarify whether the annual equivalent display should use the multiplier (12×) or the stated annual minimum (Rp 4M), or add copy acknowledging the modal factor.

## Loyalty bonus calculation semantics

The calculator computes loyalty bonuses as:
```javascript
var loyal10 = annualKontribusi * 0.5;
var loyal25 = annualKontribusi * 7.0;
```

Where `annualKontribusi = kontribusi * multiplier` (the user's current per-period input × frequency). The product documentation consistently states the bonus basis is "kontribusi dasar tahun pertama." The calculator doesn't label it as a year-1-specific figure, and nothing prevents a user from entering a higher contribution they plan to use only from year 3 onward. The FAQ answer on loyalty states "bayar kontribusi tepat waktu selama 7 tahun pertama" as the qualification criteria, further implying the year-1 figure is the relevant one.

The fix is labeling: the result row should say "50% dari kontribusi tahun pertama" rather than just "Tahun ke-10 (50%)", making the assumption explicit.

## Static COI reference inside dynamic output

The fourth result card in `missionResultsWrap` is fully static:
```html
<div class="mission-result-header tangerine">Referensi COI (Usia 30, per 1M UP)</div>
```

It contains hardcoded monthly COI values (Rp 128,300 for base, Rp 143,300 for critical care, Rp 17,700 for waiver). These never change regardless of input. Placing them inside the results wrapper that toggles `display:none`/`display:block` means they only appear after valid input, which creates the visual association with the user's specific calculation. A user entering Rp 20M/year contribution and Rp 500M UP might assume the Rp 128,300/month COI applies to their scenario, when in reality COI for a 1B UP policy at age 40 would be substantially different.

## Contribution frequency multiplier vs product equivalence

The page describes the product in two inconsistent ways:

1. The FAQ states: "Minimal premi MiSSION adalah Rp 4.000.000 per tahun (sekitar Rp 340.000 per bulan jika bayar bulanan)" — implying equivalence
2. The calculator uses `annualMultiplier.Bulanan = 12`, so 340,000 × 12 = 4,080,000 — not 4,000,000

Both the table and the Sample source confirm Rp 340,000 as the actual monthly minimum. The calculator is technically correct in multiplying by 12 for the annual equivalent. But the FAQ's "sekitar" language and the hero stat "Rp 4jt/thn" create an expectation of exact equivalence that the calculator contradicts when it displays "Kontribusi Tahunan (Ekuivalen): Rp 4.080.000" for a monthly user. The product genuinely has a Rp 80K modal factor for monthly payment — the marketing copy should either acknowledge this or the calculator should display "~Rp 4 juta" for the minimum case.
